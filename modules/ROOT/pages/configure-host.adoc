Your organization is considered the host partner in Anypoint Partner Manager.
Within the Host page, which is named after your organization, you can do the following:

* Add B2B identifiers to uniquely identify yourself with your partners.
* Add certificates owned by your organization.
* Configure global validation, acknowledgement, control number, and character set settings.

== Configure the host

. Open Partner Manager.
. From within your Partner Manager environment, select the name of your organization from the left-hand navigation.
+

* B2B Identifiers such as the AS2 Identifier, X12 ISA, X12 GS, or DUNS.

* Sending, Receiving, and Partner Endpoints - There are three types of endpoints within AnyPoint Partner Manager:

** From the Host side, you need ones to receive transmissions from your partners (where you route messages from your partners to a target at your backend systems).
+
** Sources of messages from your backend system that you send to your partners.
+
** From the Partner side, youâ€™ll want information from where you send transmissions to your partners - including acknowledgments.

* Digital certificates (such as an X.509 certificate in PKCS12 file format for the AS2 transport protocol).

* EDI message type preferences for your organization.

* Any Dataweave transformation maps your organization uses for EDI.

* Storage for your B2B message payloads.

Refer to X12 Settings to change any of the default settings for all X12 received messages.

When you are ready, you can Configure your partner(s).
